Tawny Owl

The eyes of an owl are not true “eyeballs.” Their tube-shaped eyes are completely immobile, providing binocular vision which fully focuses on their prey and boosts depth perception.

  • Scientific name: Strix aluco
  • Average Mass: 385 - 800 g
  • Average Length: 37 - 46 cm
  • Habitats: Deciduous and mixed forests

The tawny owl or brown owl is a stocky, medium-sized owl commonly found in woodlands across much of Eurasia. Its underparts are pale with dark streaks, and the upperparts are either brown or grey. This owl is non-migratory and highly territorial.
